# USA 4-Phase Periodization Model

## Overview

The USA school divides macrocycles into 4 distinct phases, each with specific stimulus goals and volume/intensity profiles:

1. **Acumulación** (Accumulation) — Build work capacity + technique foundation
2. **Desarrollo** (Development) → Transmutation — Neurological bridge fuerza to velocity
3. **Fuerza** (Strength) → Intensificación — Focused max strength + power
4. **Realización** (Realization) → Peaking — Competition-ready specificity

---

## Phase 1: Acumulación

**Duration:** 4-8 weeks
**Intensity Range:** 60-75% 1RM
**RPE Range:** 6.0-7.0
**Weekly Volume:** 400-500 reps (absolute volume high, relative intensity low)
**Training Frequency:** 2-3 days/week snatch/C&J
**Ratio Strength:Olympic:** 50:50

### Key Characteristics
- High series count (5-6 reps per set)
- Emphasis on **hang positions + block starts** (no floor variants in early weeks)
- **Strict technique enforcement** — ANY technical failure terminates set
- GPP (General Physical Preparedness) emphasis: back squats 3-4×/week, prioritize posterior chain
- Unilateral work to address imbalances
- Recovery-focused: emphasis on sarcoplasmic hypertrophy + capillarization

### Exercise Selection
**Olympic Lifts:**
- High Hang Snatch (60-70%)
- Snatch from blocks (70-75%)
- Power Clean (70-75%)
- Front Squat (65-75%)
- Back Squat (65-75%)

**Accessories:**
- Good mornings
- Deficit deadlifts (light)
- Pause squats (front + back)
- Rows (bent-over + seal rows)
- Core: dead bugs, pallof press, ab wheel

### Phase Goal
Build aerobic base + structural resilience. Prepare CNS for intensity phases.

---

## Phase 2: Desarrollo (Transmutation)

**Duration:** 4-8 weeks
**Intensity Range:** 70-82% 1RM
**RPE Range:** 7.0-8.0
**Weekly Volume:** 300-400 reps
**Training Frequency:** 3 days/week snatch/C&J
**Ratio Strength:Olympic:** 40:60

### Key Characteristics
- **Complexity escalation:** Transition from blocks → full lifts
- Neurological bridge: fuerza → ballistic velocity
- **Complexes** as primary training tool (e.g., Power Clean + Front Squat + Jerk)
- Isometric pauses at critical positions (dip, receiving, mid-thigh pull)
- Front squat takes priority over back squat (specificity to C&J)
- Moderate RPE (7.0-8.0) with crisp bar velocity emphasis

### Exercise Selection
**Olympic Lifts:**
- Snatch from blocks (75-82%)
- Snatch from floor (70-80%)
- Power Snatch (70-80%)
- Clean from blocks (75-82%)
- Clean from floor (70-80%)
- Power Clean (75-82%)
- Complexes (Power Clean + Front Squat + Jerk, etc.)

**Pauses:**
- Snatch pause (1-2s) at hip, knee, or receiving position
- Clean pause at hip, chest, or receiving position
- Front Squat pause (2s) at bottom (builds stability)

**Accessories:**
- Push Press (75-85%)
- Incline Bench (for anterior delt strength)
- Pause Front Squats
- Pin Presses (at eye level)

### Phase Goal
Develop Olympic lift proficiency + explosive strength under load. Neurological adaptation to velocity.

---

## Phase 3: Fuerza (Strength/Intensificación)

**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Intensity Range:** 80-92% 1RM
**RPE Range:** 8.5-9.0
**Weekly Volume:** 200-300 reps (low volume)
**Training Frequency:** 3 days/week snatch/C&J
**Ratio Strength:Olympic:** 30:70

### Key Characteristics
- **Maximum strength focus** with special emphasis on weak positions
- Accommodating resistance (bands, chains) — Mash methodology
- Doubles + singles predominate
- Deload week (sem 4): -40% volume, maintenance intensity
- Elimination of isolating accessories (focus on compound movements only)
- Back Squat 2×/week for CNS recovery stimulus (heavy but sub-maximal)

### Exercise Selection
**Olympic Lifts:**
- Snatch singles (85-92%)
- Clean & Jerk singles (85-92%)
- Power Snatches (80-88%, explosive)
- Power Cleans (80-88%, explosive)
- Pulls (105% of best Snatch/Clean) — 3×3-5 in high-frequency weeks

**Accommodation Methods:**
- Bands: +15-20% band tension at top
- Chains: 30-50% of barbell loaded from chains
- Pin Press: 3-6 inches from chest (acute angle)
- Deficit Deadlifts (2-4 inches) to address positional weakness

**Squats:**
- Back Squat 2-3 reps @ 85-90% (twice/week)
- Front Squat 2-3 reps @ 85-88% (once/week)
- Pin Squat (at knee or above) @ 90-95%

### Phase Goal
Maximize relative strength. Fortify weak points in pull + squat. Prepare nervous system for competition.

---

## Phase 4: Realización (Peaking/Competition)

**Duration:** 2-3 weeks
**Intensity Range:** 85-100%+ 1RM
**RPE Range:** 9.0-10.0
**Weekly Volume:** <150 reps (minimal, highly specific)
**Training Frequency:** 4-5 days/week snatch/C&J
**Ratio Strength:Olympic:** 15:85

### Key Characteristics
- **Total Olympic specificity** — All lifts from floor (no blocks/hangs)
- Singles daily (builds CNS frequency without fatigue)
- Squats for **recovery maintenance only** (light, high-frequency)
- Within 10-14 days of competition: 95-100%+ attempts
- Competition simulation: full C&J + Snatch sequences
- Deload 48 hours pre-competition (60-70% intensity, technique focus)

### Exercise Selection
**Olympic Lifts (ONLY):**
- Snatch singles @ 95-100%+
- Clean singles @ 95-100%+
- Jerk singles @ 95-100%+
- Full sequence simulations (Snatch + rest + C&J)

**Squat Maintenance (light):**
- Recovery Back Squat 3×5 @ 60-65% (technique, stability)
- Recovery Front Squat 2×5 @ 60-65%

**NO Accessories**
(All non-Olympic work eliminated)

### Phase Goal
Mental confidence + competitive readiness. Peaking realization = competition day performance.

---

## Deload & Recovery Weeks

**Placement:** Weeks 4, 8, 12 in standard macrocycles

**Structure:**
- Volume: -40-60% compared to prior week
- Intensity: Maintain or slight reduction (-5%)
- RPE: 6.0-7.0 (intentionally low)
- Focus: Technique, mobility, recovery

**Rationale:** Allows CNS supercompensation + glycogen repletion. Prevents overtraining while maintaining adaptation stimulus.
