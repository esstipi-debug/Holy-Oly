# USA School Coaching Philosophy & Cues

Extracted coaching principles and biomechanical cues from consolidated USA methodology (USAW, Catalyst, Juggernaut, Mash, California Strength).

---

## Core Philosophy

### 1. Technical Mastery First
- "Prohibit technical failure" — Mash & Catalyst principle
- Better 10 perfect reps @ 70% than 3 sloppy reps @ 85%
- Technique quality gates intensity progression
- Early phases (Acumulación) enforce zero-tolerance failures

### 2. Individualized Auto-Regulation
- RPE > Prescribed Load
- Athletes monitor readiness (HRV, sleep, mood) daily
- RPE 6-7 in Acumulación should feel "doable"; RPE 9 in Fuerza should be deliberate max effort
- Juggernaut's "rate of perceived exertion" replaces rigid % prescriptions

### 3. Periodization = Neurological Adaptation
- Phase progression = increasing specificity to competition movements
- Early phases: high volume, low intensity (build aerobic base + neural efficiency)
- Late phases: low volume, high intensity (competition specificity + CNS frequency)
- Mash: "Periodization is the progressive assault on the nervous system"

### 4. Respect the Sport's Demands
- Snatch + Clean & Jerk are explosive, ballistic, technical lifts
- Cannot be done "slowly" or with poor positioning
- Blocks/hangs in early phases = safer entry point (reduces injury risk)
- Floor variants only when technical proficiency proven

## Catalyst Athletics (Greg Everett) Cues

### Snatch

**Setup & Stance**
- "Neutral spine, shoulders over bar"
- "Feet hip-width, toes slightly out (5-15 degrees)"
- "Hands in snatch position — shoulder width or slightly wider, depending on proportion"

**First Pull (Knees)**
- "Extend your legs first — don't squat the weight"
- "Keep chest tall, shoulders over or slightly in front of bar"
- "Knees extend first, then hips extend"
- "This isn't a squat — it's a leg extension with hip hinge"

**Transition & Acceleration**
- "Vertical bar path — prevent 'casting' forward"
- "Explosive triple extension: ankles → knees → hips"
- "Pull elbows HIGH and FAST under the bar"
- "Think: Elbows first, then drop"

**Receiving Position**
- "Catch in full-depth squat — no compromises"
- "Shoulders slightly in front of bar (active shoulder stability)"
- "Elbows high, wrists neutral or slightly extended"
- "Heels down — vertical shin (or slight forward angle)"

**Recovery**
- "Drive through legs to stand"
- "Maintain chest tall, bar over midfoot during ascent"

### Clean

**Setup (Same as Snatch — slightly narrower grip)**
- "Hands just outside leg width"
- "Shoulders over bar, spine neutral"

**First Pull**
- "Knees extend first — no early hip rise"
- "Maintain chest angle throughout first pull"
- "Elbows extended, straight arms on the bar"

**Transition & Second Pull**
- "Keep shoulders in front of bar until power position (mid-thigh)"
- "Explosive triple extension, same rhythm as snatch"
- "Pull bar vertically, but slightly closer to body than Snatch"

**Catch Position (High Pull/Power Clean)**
- "Elbows FAST under the bar — lead with elbows, not hands"
- "Chest up, shoulders stable"
- "Heels down or slight heel lift (depends on ankle mobility)"

**Receiving (Full Clean)**
- "Catch in full squat OR power + front squat dip"
- "Drive elbows forward (not lateral) in front squat dip"
- "Chest tall throughout dip and recovery"

## Juggernaut Training (Max Aita) Principles

### Volume Load Management
- RVL (Relative Volume Load) = Series × Reps × (Intensity % 1RM)
- Prescribe RVL targets, not absolute loads
- Example: "Target 50 RVL units this week" allows athlete flexibility
- Calculation: 5 sets × 2 reps × 85% = 8.5 RVL per set = 42.5 RVL total

### RPE-Based Auto-Regulation
- **RPE 6** = "Could do 4 more reps"
- **RPE 7** = "Could do 3 more reps"
- **RPE 8** = "Could do 2 more reps"
- **RPE 9** = "Could do 1 more rep" (max effort, but controlled)
- **RPE 10** = "Absolute maximum, failure imminent"

**Application:**
- Acumulación: RPE 6-7 (comfortable, controlled)
- Desarrollo: RPE 7-8 (challenging but deliberate)
- Fuerza: RPE 8-9 (maximum effort)
- Realización: RPE 9-10 (competition simulation)

### Compliance Scoring
- Track: Reps prescribed vs. reps executed, Load prescribed vs. actual load, RPE prescribed vs. actual RPE
- Score = 0.25×V_compliance + 0.55×Load_compliance + 0.20×RPE_compliance
- Athletes executing 85% of load @ 75% reps & RPE +0.5 = 92.6% compliance (acceptable)
- Allows intelligent adaptation without "failure"

## Mash Elite Performance (Travis Mash) Principles

### Accommodating Resistance
- **Bands:** Add 15-20% of load at lockout (most overloading effect where strong)
- **Chains:** 30-50% of barbell from chains (progressive overload through range)
- Rationale: Increases time-under-tension + teaches bar acceleration mid-range
- Used in Fuerza phase for max strength + positional work

### System Loading (SyL)
- Measures total neurological demand (not just volume)
- SyL = Series × Reps × Intensity × Density
- Emphasizes frequency + intensity pairing
- High frequency (4-5 days Snatch/C&J) in Realización = high SyL despite low reps

### Deload 2:1 Ratio
- For every 2-3 weeks of accumulation, 1 week deload
- Deload = 40-60% volume reduction + maintained (or reduced) intensity
- Allows homeostatic adaptation + prevents overtraining plateau

## California Strength (Dave Spitz) Principles

### Position-Specific Work
- **High Hang Snatch** (above waist) = footwork + receiving pattern practice
- **Snatch from Blocks** (knee height) = positional strength development
- **Snatch from Floor** (full range) = competition specificity
- Progression: HH → Blocks → Floor (builds stability + power transference)

### Pull Emphasis
- Snatch Pull @ 105% of best Snatch = supramaximal loading
- Clean Pull @ 105% of best Clean = maximal pulling power
- Trains bar velocity + positional accuracy under heavy load
- Used 1-2x/week in Fuerza + Realización phases

### Tempo Variation
- **Fast pulls** (explosive bar acceleration) = power development
- **Pause pulls** (1-3s at power position) = positional stability
- **Dead-stop pulls** (bar reset between reps) = eliminates stretch reflex
- Alternates weekly or within microcycle

## Phase-Specific Coaching Emphasis

### Acumulación (Focus: Technique + Volume Tolerance)
- Cue: "Quality over quantity" 
- Enforce technical gates: perfect 5 reps before load increase
- Build positional competency in hanging/block positions
- Emphasis: "This phase builds your foundation"

### Desarrollo (Focus: Explosive Power Development)
- Cue: "Speed on the bar"
- Emphasis: Bar velocity under load
- Use complexes to connect movements: Power Clean → Front Squat → Jerk
- Teach athletes to "feel" positional transitions
- Cue: "Elbows fast, drop under fast"

### Fuerza (Focus: Maximum Strength + Weak Point Development)
- Cue: "Own your weakness"
- Use pause + pin variations for weak positions
- Heavier loads, lower reps
- Frequency remains high (3×/week lift) but volume drops
- Cue: "Each rep counts — dial in positioning"

### Realización (Focus: Confidence + Competition Readiness)
- Cue: "This is YOUR number"
- Snatch + Clean & Jerk from floor ONLY
- Daily singles = confidence building
- Less feedback, more autonomy
- Cue: "You've done the work — trust the process"

## Common Cueing Themes

### Across All Phases
1. **Vertical Bar Path** — "Straight up, straight down" prevents forward drift
2. **Elbows Fast** — "Lead with elbows under the bar" (not hands)
3. **Chest Tall** — Maintains spinal integrity + shoulder stability
4. **Heels Down** — Ensures ankle mobility + full depth receiving
5. **Speed Under** — "The pull doesn't matter if you don't get under fast"

### Athlete Autonomy Language
- "How did that feel?" vs. "You did that wrong"
- "What adjustment would help?" vs. "Do it this way"
- Empowers self-awareness + builds coaching relationship

### RPE Verification
- Coach asks: "On a 6-10 scale, how hard was that set?"
- Athlete responds with RPE
- If RPE ≠ prescribed → adjust load next set OR modify rep scheme
- Compliance score captures this misalignment (no "failure," just data)
